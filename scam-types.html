Common Scams to Watch Out For (2025)

Scam Type Target Audience Warning Signs How to Protect Yourself
Cryptocurrency Investment Seniors, Veterans
  • Promises of guaranteed returns
  • Pressure to invest quickly
  • Unsolicited investment advice
  • Research before investing
  • Verify investment firm credentials
  • Never send crypto to unknown parties
Government Impersonation Veterans, Seniors
  • Calls about benefits changes
  • Threats of arrest
  • Requests for immediate payment
  • Verify caller credentials
  • Contact agencies directly
  • Never pay with gift cards
Romance Scams Seniors, Vulnerable Adults
  • Quick professions of love
  • Cannot meet in person
  • Requests for money/gifts
  • Never send money to online friends
  • Verify profile authenticity
  • Share concerns with family
Tech Support Scams Seniors
  • Pop-up virus warnings
  • Unsolicited tech support calls
  • Requests for remote access
  • Don't click pop-up warnings
  • Never give remote access
  • Contact official support only

Remember:

If something sounds too good to be true, it probably is. Always verify before you trust, and never make rushed decisions about money or personal information.
